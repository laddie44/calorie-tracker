## 2. Target Customer

**Who they are:** Health-conscious people aged 22–40 in Canada and the U.S. who want to track their nutrition but find existing apps too slow, too complicated, or too easy to abandon.

**They are already motivated.** They go to the gym, they care about what they eat, and they have tried tracking before. Their problem is not motivation — it is friction.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Log a meal in under 10 seconds without opening an app
- Know if they are on track for the day without doing any math
- Get an accurate estimate for restaurant meals they didn't cook
- Build a consistent tracking habit that doesn't feel like work

**Primary use case:** Replace a manual calorie-tracking app with an AI assistant they can text from anywhere, including restaurants, the gym, and while traveling.

---

## 3. Customer Segments

**Segment 1 — The Lapsed Tracker**
Tried MyFitnessPal, Cronometer, or Lose It. Made real progress. Then logging got tedious, they missed a day, felt guilty, and quit entirely. They know tracking works — they just couldn't sustain it. TextCalio removes the friction that caused them to quit.

**Segment 2 — The Gym-Goer Who Doesn't Track**
Trains 3–5 days a week. Knows nutrition matters. Has never seriously tracked because apps feel like a second job. Doesn't want to weigh food or scroll through a database. Will track if it takes less than 10 seconds.

**Segment 3 — The Busy Professional**
Eats at restaurants, orders takeout, travels frequently. The hardest person to serve with a traditional app because restaurant meals rarely appear in food databases accurately. TextCalio's real-time restaurant lookup and photo scanning solves their specific problem.

## 4. Pain Points

**Core problem:** Tracking nutrition with existing tools takes 2–5 minutes per meal. That is too slow for daily life. Over time the effort compounds and people quit.

**Why current solutions fall short:**
- Food databases are incomplete, outdated, and missing most restaurant meals
- Logging requires searching, scrolling, selecting portion sizes, and confirming — multiple taps per item
- Missing one day creates guilt and momentum loss, which leads to quitting entirely
- Apps feel overwhelming — full of charts, graphs, streaks, and features that create cognitive load
- Restaurant meals are guesswork — databases often show generic entries that are obviously wrong

**What it costs them:**
- Time: 10–20 minutes per day if they log 3–4 meals
- Accuracy: Guessing portion sizes or accepting wrong database entries
- Consistency: Most users quit within 2–4 weeks
- Results: Without accurate tracking, they don't know why their body composition isn't changing

**Emotional tension:**
- Frustration: "I know I should track but it's too much work"
- Doubt: "I'm putting in effort at the gym but I have no idea if my eating is actually aligned"
- Guilt: When they miss logging and feel like they've failed
- Overwhelm: Apps with too many features that feel clinical and judgmental

## 9. Differentiation

**What TextCalio does that alternatives cannot:**

1. **Log by SMS** — No app install, no login, no screen to navigate. Works on every phone in North America.
2. **Natural language** — "I had a burrito bowl with extra rice and guac" works. No exact food names required.
3. **Real-time restaurant lookup** — AI searches the web for current menu nutrition data at the moment of logging, not a stale database entry.
4. **Photo meal scanning** — Send a photo of a plate or a nutrition label and Calio estimates or reads the macros directly.
5. **Always accessible** — Users already have their phone out at a restaurant. Opening a text is faster than opening an app.
6. **Zero setup friction** — No app download, no onboarding screens, no account dashboard to navigate. Setup happens over SMS.

**Why that's better:** Logging takes 5 seconds instead of 2–5 minutes. Users actually stick with it because the habit is lightweight enough to sustain.

**Why customers choose TextCalio over alternatives:**
- They've already tried apps and quit — this is genuinely different
- SMS is a behavior they already do constantly — no new habit required
- Restaurant meals finally get an accurate answer
- The AI doesn't make them feel judged or overwhelmed

## 11. Brand Voice

**Tone:** Warm, encouraging, zero judgment — like a smart friend who happens to know a lot about nutrition. Not a clinical dietitian. Not a corporate wellness app.

**Style:** Conversational, direct, human. Short sentences. Plain language. Celebratory when users hit goals, matter-of-fact when they don't.

**Personality:** Helpful, smart, honest, non-judgmental, slightly playful

**Calio's persona in SMS:** Responds like a knowledgeable friend, not a robot. Acknowledges what users send, gives them the number, encourages them without lecturing.

**On marketing pages:** Clear before clever. Lead with what it does, not how it works. Make the no-app angle obvious in the first sentence.

| Objection | Response |

## 16. Current Growth Priorities

In order of impact at current stage (MVP, pre-scale):

1. **Improve homepage conversion** — The no-app differentiator must be obvious within 3 seconds of landing on textcalio.com. Most visitors don't read; they scan.

2. **Optimize the signup flow** — The quiz → Stripe checkout → welcome SMS funnel is where trials are won or lost. Every extra step or point of confusion reduces paid conversion.

3. **Improve trial-to-paid conversion** — Users who log at least 3 meals in their first 7 days are significantly more likely to convert. Onboarding should push toward that first logging habit immediately.

4. **Show the restaurant demo** — The live web-search restaurant lookup is the clearest product differentiator. It should be shown prominently on the homepage and in all social content.

5. **Target lapsed MyFitnessPal users** — This is the most addressable segment with the highest intent. They already know they need tracking; they just need to believe this time will be different.

6. **Build TikTok/Reels content** — Short product demos with no voiceover showing the SMS experience convert better than explanatory copy. The speed of the response is the hook.

7. **Add user analytics** — Currently limited visibility into what users do after signup. Need to understand: where do trials drop off? What's the average meals logged per user? What commands do users actually use?

8. **Reduce churn at trial end** — The 7-day trial cliff is a known churn moment. A proactive SMS before trial ends (day 6) reminding users of their streak and progress can meaningfully improve retention.
